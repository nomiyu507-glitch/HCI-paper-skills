# Field Adjustments

Use this reference only when the task depends on disciplinary conventions.

## HCI

Optimize for:

- clear problem framing in human context
- relation to prior HCI paradigms, methods, or interactions
- explicit contribution type: empirical finding, system, method, framework, dataset, or design implication
- balanced claims, especially around user studies and qualitative findings

Watch for:

- benchmark-style overclaiming without user relevance
- weak articulation of why the work matters to HCI
- method sections that omit study design rationale

## Design Research

Optimize for:

- artifact/process/context clarity
- reflection and knowledge contribution
- why this design case generalizes or transfers
- relation between making, evaluation, and argument

Watch for:

- overly engineering-style writing that hides design reasoning
- vague contribution language such as "innovative" without evidence
- polished artifact description without reflective insight

## CS

Optimize for:

- sharp problem statement
- precise terminology
- fair baselines and evaluation design
- limitations stated without self-sabotage

Watch for:

- missing ablations where they are expected
- unsupported novelty claims
- disconnected related work and method framing
